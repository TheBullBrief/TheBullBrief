Small-Cap Stocks Outshine as Investors Rotate Away from Tech Giants

By Vinay Yerramsetti | September 18, 2024

A notable rotation is underway in the stock market as investors shift their focus from large-cap growth stocks, particularly those dominated by the tech sector, to small-cap equities. The Russell 2000, which tracks small-cap companies, has outperformed the S&P 500 this quarter, signaling increased interest in undervalued opportunities in smaller companies.

This shift reflects a broader sentiment that small-cap stocks may be better positioned to benefit from the Federal Reserveâ€™s recent interest rate cut. With lower borrowing costs, many smaller companies are expected to leverage debt more effectively, making them attractive to investors seeking higher returns amid the changing monetary landscape.

Experts caution, however, that small-cap stocks can be volatile, and investors should weigh the potential risks against the opportunity for outsized gains. The coming months will reveal whether this rotation marks a lasting trend or a short-term response to macroeconomic shifts.
